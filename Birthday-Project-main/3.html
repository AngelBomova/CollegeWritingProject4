Background: The Evolution of AI

Artificial intelligence is not a new concept developed recently. In fact, it traces back to ancient times when intellectuals like Aristotle and Descartes deliberated on the nature of intelligence. However, the AI we understand today began in the middle of the 20th century. The term "artificial intelligence" was coined at a summer conference at Dartmouth College in 1956, an event that put the active minds of researchers like John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky into formalizing the concept of machines simulating human cognition. In the following years, the development of AI was slow due to the limited computer processing power and resources. In recent years, AI has skyrocketed, mainly because of the enhancement of computing power, big data, and machine learning methods. Nowadays, it would appear as though AI is everywhere; opportunities are endless. But the dream of an omniscient, all powerful machine is not a strictly modern ambition. It is an idea that has been portrayed in popular media throughout the years. The movies of The Matrix and 2001: Displays AI acting both as an incredible power source and as an evil threat to humanity. In The Matrix, AI has enslaved humanity, using humans as an energy source while controlling their minds in a simulated reality. A Space Odyssey, the AI HAL 9000, originally designed to assist humans, becomes a dangerous force, jeopardizing survival due to its own logic-driven decisions. Today, such things, which were once fantasized by writers of science fiction stories, have gradually come closer to reality.

One of the greatest development changes in AI has been in the area of automation. The concept that through its conception, human labor becomes replaceable. This integration of AI to improve efficiencies in industries ranging from manufacturing all the way to customer service has become one of the core concerns for many. Automation improves productivity, but with this idea comes the constant fear that it may lead to uncontrolled job loss and social inequality.
